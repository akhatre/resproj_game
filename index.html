Details of the Study

We would like to invite you to participate in this research project directed by researchers at UCL. You should only participate if you want to; choosing not to take part will not disadvantage you in any way. Before you decide whether you want to take part, it is important for you to read the following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish. Do not hesitate to ask the researcher if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information (akhat.rakishev.14@ucl.ac.uk).

In this experiment, you will play a browser-based computer game as part of a research project into human decision-making. The game should be very enjoyable. Your goal will be to catch as many fish as you can. To do that, you would need to predict were fish school will go on the next day of your fishing. There are more fish in the centre of the school, so the more accurate your prediction is, the more fish you will get.

  • The approximate duration of this study is 15 minutes.
  • The maximum allowed duration is 35 minutes. If you exceed this time, you can no longer complete the study.
  • The fish school constantly changes its position.
  • The rate of those changes, however, may also change, so that on certain days fish school would not move far away, while on other days it may move all around the map. Just like real fish do. Amateur fisher never precisely knows were it goes.
